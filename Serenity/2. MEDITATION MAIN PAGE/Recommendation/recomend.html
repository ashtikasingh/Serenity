BOOKS

Twenty years ago, finding resources on meditation was far more difficult. However, now you have the ability to jump online and find information on anything you’d want related to the practice within a matter of seconds. Despite this, many of the greatest resources on meditation are still books, many of which were written long before the Internet was ever around.

  • Making Space by Thich Nhat Hanh
  • Zen Mind, Beginner’s Mind by Shunryu Suzuki
  • The Miracle of Mindfulness by Thich Nhat Hanh
  • 10% Happier by Dan Harris
  • Out of Your Mind by Alan Watts

PODCASTS

When life gets chaotic, finding time to sit down, let alone meditate, may seem unrealistic. The good news? You don’t need hours or even much experience to benefit from a daily practice of meditation. Being able to bring awareness to your breath and refocus your attention on the present moment can help you manage stress, reduce anxiety, and feel more energized. There are plenty of great reasons to listen to meditation podcasts.

  • The OneMind Podcast with Morgan Dix
  • The Mindful Minute Podcast
  • The Daily Meditation Podcast
  • Meditation Oasis Podcast
  • Meditation Minis Podcast
  • Roaming Yogis Meditation Podcast

GAMES

What are the most relaxing games you should play to help you de-stress? Sometimes we don’t always want to end a long, busy day with the head-pumping rhythm game violence of Thumper or north of 100 attempts against a brutal Dark Souls boss. Instead, there are times when all we want to do is nestle into our cosy gaming thrones with a cup of tea and a nice chill game to help us forget our stresses and strains.

  • Ping Pong
  • Tetris
  • Rock, Paper & Scissors
  • Snake
  • Flappy Bird
  • Bubble Shooter
